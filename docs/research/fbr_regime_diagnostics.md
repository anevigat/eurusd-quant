# FBR Regime Diagnostics (Frozen Configuration)

## Objective

Diagnose when the frozen `false_breakout_reversal` setup performs well or poorly across market regimes.

This research is **diagnostic only**:

- no strategy logic changes
- no parameter optimization
- no new filters
- no entry/exit redesign

## Frozen Strategy Context

- `strategy`: `false_breakout_reversal`
- `entry window`: `08:00-09:00 UTC`
- `allowed_side`: `both`
- `exit_model`: `atr_target`

## Features Analyzed

Entry-time regime features:

- Asian range size (`asian_high - asian_low`, 00:00-06:00 UTC)
- Volatility proxy (`ATR(14)` on mid prices at entry bar)
- Spread at entry (`spread_open`)
- Distance from Asian midpoint (`|entry_price - asian_midpoint|`)
- Pre-London trend (`mid_close(last 06:00-08:00) - mid_open(first 06:00-08:00)`)
- Day of week
- Month
- Trade side (`long` / `short`)

## Segmentation Results (Highlights)

### Quantile Diagnostics

| feature | best segment (net_pnl) | worst segment (net_pnl) |
|---|---:|---:|
| asian_range_quantile | Q3 (`+0.024546`) | Q5 (`-0.021101`) |
| volatility_quantile | Q3 (`+0.003273`) | Q2 (`-0.004706`) |
| spread_quantile | Q1 (`+0.014965`) | Q2 (`-0.014624`) |

Interpretation:

- Strategy is strongest in **mid-range Asian ranges (Q3)** and weakest in **very wide Asian ranges (Q5)**.
- Lower spreads (Q1) are materially more favorable than higher spread buckets.
- Volatility effect exists but is weaker/non-monotonic versus range/spread effects.

### Calendar / Side Effects

| segment | net_pnl | notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Thursday | `+0.011454` | strongest weekday in sample |
| Tuesday | `-0.019626` | weakest weekday in sample |
| February | `+0.021321` | strongest month in sample |
| August | `-0.019508` | weakest month in sample |
| Long side | `-0.028272` | underperforms materially |

### Pre-London Trend Context

| pre_london_trend_direction | trade_count | net_pnl |
|---|---:|---:|
| down | 494 | `+0.003495` |
| up | 518 | `-0.017178` |
| flat | 2 | `+0.001294` (insufficient sample) |

Interpretation:

- Reversal performance is weaker when 06:00-08:00 trend is already up, and modestly better after pre-London downside drift.

## Yearly Robustness Context

From `yearly_performance.csv`:

- Positive years: `2019, 2022, 2023, 2024`
- Negative years: `2018, 2020, 2021`

Regime diagnostics suggest this year-to-year instability is associated with shifts in range/spread/trend mix rather than one single scalar factor.

## Conclusion

The frozen configuration appears **regime-dependent**:

- favorable under moderate Asian ranges and lower spreads
- unfavorable in wider-range and some weekday/month contexts
- short side dominates long side in this sample

This analysis does not attempt to improve the strategy; it isolates where current behavior is likely condition-sensitive.
